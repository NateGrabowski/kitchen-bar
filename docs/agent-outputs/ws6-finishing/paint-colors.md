# Paint Color Research

**Work Stream:** WS6 - Finishing & Aesthetics
**Date:** 2026-01-09
**Focus:** Navy and charcoal cabinet paint options for modern farmhouse aesthetic

---

## Executive Summary

Based on the project's modern farmhouse aesthetic with warm wood accents (chevron acacia butcher block, X-frame panels, barn door), **Sherwin Williams Iron Ore (charcoal)** or **Benjamin Moore Hale Navy** are the top recommendations. Both pair beautifully with warm wood tones and create the sophisticated contrast needed for this design.

---

## Navy Paint Options

### 1. Benjamin Moore Hale Navy (HC-154) - TOP RECOMMENDATION

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Brand** | Benjamin Moore |
| **Color Code** | HC-154 |
| **Color Name** | Hale Navy |
| **LRV** | 6.3 (very dark) |
| **Undertones** | Gray undertones (safer, more neutral) |
| **Best Finish** | Satin or Semi-Gloss |

**Why It Works:**
- "Classic navy blue that gives a sense of balance, stability and reassurance" (Benjamin Moore)
- Gray undertones prevent it from becoming overwhelming
- Pairs exceptionally well with warm wood tones, brass hardware, and natural materials
- Benjamin Moore's #1 selling navy for decades
- Safe choice - looks sophisticated in any lighting

**Sample Chip:** Benjamin Moore color chip HC-154

## Charcoal/Dark Gray Paint Options

### 1. Sherwin Williams Iron Ore (SW 7069) - TOP RECOMMENDATION

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Brand** | Sherwin Williams |
| **Color Code** | SW 7069 |
| **Color Name** | Iron Ore |
| **LRV** | 6 (very dark) |
| **Undertones** | Slight blue undertones, dramatic |
| **Best Finish** | Satin or Semi-Gloss |

**Why It Works:**
- Rich, deep charcoal with incredible depth
- Slight blue undertones prevent harshness
- Looks more black in low light, more gray/charcoal in bright light
- Popular for cabinets, doors, accent walls
- Sophisticated without being stark black

**Designer Note:** Iron Ore is one of the most requested dark paint colors for modern farmhouse kitchens.

## Sheen Recommendation

### Satin Finish (Recommended)

| Pro | Con |
|-----|-----|
| Hides brush strokes better | Slightly less durable than semi-gloss |
| Forgiving on imperfections | May show fingerprints slightly more |
| Modern, sophisticated look | - |
| Easier DIY application | - |

### Semi-Gloss Finish (Alternative)

| Pro | Con |
|-----|-----|
| Most durable | Shows imperfections more |
| Best moisture resistance | May highlight brush strokes |
| Easy to wipe clean | Can look too shiny |
| Reflects light well | - |

**Recommendation:** Use **Satin** for cabinet bodies. For high-touch areas like drawer fronts, semi-gloss is acceptable if preferred durability is desired.

---

## Paint Product Recommendations

### For Navy Colors

**Option A: Benjamin Moore Advance (Alkyd/Waterborne)**
- Self-leveling, beautiful finish
- Slow dry time (allow 16+ hours between coats)
- Best for brush/roller application
- Price: ~$80/gallon

**Option B: Sherwin Williams Emerald Urethane Trim Enamel**
- Faster drying than Advance
- Excellent durability
- Self-leveling
- Price: ~$95/gallon

### For Charcoal Colors

Same product recommendations apply. Both Advance and Emerald Urethane work excellently with darker colors.
