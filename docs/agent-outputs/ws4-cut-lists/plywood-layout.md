# Plywood Sheet Layout Diagrams

**Project:** Kitchen Bar Cabinet
**Date:** 2026-01-09
**Purpose:** Visual cutting diagrams to minimize waste and optimize plywood usage

---

## Overview

This document shows HOW to cut pieces from standard 4' × 8' (48" × 96") plywood sheets.

**Key principles:**
- RIP CUTS FIRST (full-length cuts with grain)
- CROSSCUTS SECOND (shorter cuts across grain)
- Minimize waste by nesting similar-sized pieces
- Account for ⅛" kerf per cut

**Sheets needed:**
- 3 sheets ¾" cabinet-grade plywood (A-grade one face, paint-grade)
- 1 sheet ½" plywood (drawer boxes)
- 1 sheet ¼" plywood (backs and drawer bottoms)

### Cutting Sequence (Sheet 1)

**Step 1: Have store rip to 14" strips**
- Request: Three strips at 14" × 96"
- Store cuts are free or ~$1 per cut
- Brings home manageable pieces

**Step 2: Crosscut sides at home (miter saw)**
- From first 14" × 96" strip: Cut A1 (34.5") and A2 (34.5")
- From second 14" × 96" strip: Cut B1 (34.5") and B2 (34.5")
- From third 14" × 96" strip: Cut C1 (34.5") and C2 (34.5")
- Remaining from each strip: ~60" for tops/bottoms/shelves

**Step 3: Crosscut tops, bottoms, and shelves**
- From remaining strips, crosscut to widths:
  - A3, A4: 28.5" each
  - B3, B4: 28.5" each
  - C3, C4: 30.5" each
  - B5, B6: 28.5" each

**Pieces from Sheet 1:**
- 6 cabinet sides ✓
- 3 cabinet tops ✓
- 3 cabinet bottoms ✓
- 2 shelves ✓

**Waste:** ~10-12 sq ft of usable offcuts for other parts


### Cutting Sequence (Sheet 2)

**Step 1: Cut barn door panel (circular saw + guide)**
- Mark 30" × 35" rectangle on sheet
- GOOD FACE DOWN (saw tearout on bottom)
- Score cut line with utility knife
- Clamp straightedge guide
- Cut barn door panel (BD)

**Step 2: Cut drawer fronts strip**
- From remaining sheet, rip a 10.5" wide strip
- Crosscut three drawer fronts at 30" each (DF1, DF2, DF3)

**Step 3: Cut cabinet doors**
- Rip 16" wide strip (slightly oversize for two doors)
- Crosscut to 33" length
- Rip each door to final 15⅝" width
- Results in DOOR-L and DOOR-R

**Step 4: Cut remaining shelf**
- From offcuts, cut B6 shelf at 28.5" × 13"

**Pieces from Sheet 2:**
- 1 barn door panel ✓
- 3 drawer fronts ✓
- 2 cabinet doors ✓
- 1 shelf (B6) ✓

**Waste:** ~8-10 sq ft

### Cutting Sequence (Sheet 3)

**Critical:** This sheet has the focal-point living room panel. Take extra care.

**Step 1: Have store rough-cut (optional but recommended)**
- Request one crosscut at 40" (separates panel area from toe kick area)
- Brings home two pieces: 48" × 40" and 48" × 56"

**Step 2: Cut living room panel (circular saw + guide)**
- From 48" × 96" section (or 48" × 56" if pre-cut)
- Mark 92" × 34.5" on GOOD FACE
- Score cut lines with utility knife
- Place GOOD FACE DOWN on sawhorses
- Clamp straightedge guide for each cut
- Cut length to 92" first
- Cut width to 34.5" second
- **Double-check squareness** - this is the most visible piece

**Step 3: Cut toe kick top (from remaining piece)**
- Mark 92" × 14" rectangle
- Cut to size
- This piece less critical (hidden under cabinet)

**Step 4: Save offcuts**
- Remaining pieces good for testing finish, router practice, etc.

**Pieces from Sheet 3:**
- 1 living room panel (92" × 34.5") ✓
- 1 toe kick platform top (92" × 14") ✓

**Waste:** ~5 sq ft

## Store Cutting Strategy

### What to Have Store Cut (Saves You Time and Effort)

**Big box stores (Home Depot, Lowe's) offer cutting services:**
- First 1-2 cuts free
- Additional cuts: $0.25-0.50 each
- Worth it for straight, long rip cuts

**Recommended store cuts:**

**¾" Sheet 1:**
- Rip into three 14" × 96" strips (3 cuts = ~$1.50)

**½" Sheet 1:**
- Rip into three 10" × 96" strips (3 cuts = ~$1.50)

**¾" Sheet 3 (optional):**
- Crosscut at 40" to separate living room panel area (1 cut = $0.50)

**¼" Sheet 1 (optional):**
- Rough cut three back panels (~3 cuts = ~$1.50)

**Total store cutting cost: ~$5-7**

**What you save:**
- Long rip cuts with circular saw (difficult to get straight)
- Handling full 4×8 sheets at home
- Time: ~2-3 hours of cutting and setup

**What you cut at home:**
- All crosscuts (miter saw - easy and accurate)
- Large panels (circular saw + guide - manageable)
- Final trim cuts

## Cutting Safety Reminders

**When cutting large panels:**
- [ ] Always have support on both sides of cut (sawhorses or helper)
- [ ] Score cut line with utility knife (prevents tearout)
- [ ] Good face DOWN when using circular saw (tearout on bottom)
- [ ] Clamp straightedge guide securely
- [ ] Let saw reach full speed before entering cut
- [ ] Keep hands clear of blade path
- [ ] Wear safety glasses and hearing protection

**When using miter saw:**
- [ ] Secure piece against fence
- [ ] Use stop block for repeated cuts (ensures consistency)
- [ ] Keep hands 6" away from blade
- [ ] Let blade stop completely before removing piece

## Optimization Notes

### Why This Layout is Efficient

1. **Groups similar cuts together** - All 14" wide pieces cut from same rip
2. **Minimizes sheet handling** - Store rips reduce full-sheet wrestling
3. **Reduces waste** - Nests pieces efficiently
4. **Accounts for kerf** - ⅛" kerf factored into layouts
5. **Prioritizes critical pieces** - Living room panel gets dedicated sheet

### Waste Reduction Tips

**Use offcuts for:**
- Shelf supports
- Shims and spacers
- Test pieces for pocket holes, dados, finish
- Drawer dividers
- Backing for X-frame trim

**Don't throw away:**
- Any piece larger than 12" × 12"
- Long strips (good for face frames, trim)
- Clean-edged scraps (can become small shelves)
