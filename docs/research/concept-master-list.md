## Category C: Structural Variations

*Focus: Different CONSTRUCTION approaches - cabinet location, bar shape, layout*

| # | Concept | Status | Boldness | Description | Trade-offs |
|---|---------|--------|----------|-------------|------------|
| C1 | **Continuous Cabinet (Baseline)** | SKETCHED | Standard | Cabinet runs from bar top to LR floor (63.5" tall) | Max storage, custom build required |
| C2 | **Kitchen-Floor Cabinets** | SKETCHED | Standard | Standard 30-36" cabinets on kitchen floor; step = decorative panel | `concept-kitchen-floor-cabinets.jsx` |
| C3 | Hybrid Split Cabinet | AVAILABLE | Moderate | Upper cabinet on kitchen floor + separate lower storage in step | Two zones, mixed materials, more complex |

| C5 | Pure Bar (No Cabinet) | AVAILABLE | Bold | Just bar top with open space below - zero storage | Maximum openness, loses all storage |
| C6 | **Angled/L-Shape Bar** | SKETCHED | Moderate | Bar wraps corner or angles instead of straight 84" | `concept-angled-bar.jsx` |
| C7 | Terraced Multi-Level | AVAILABLE | Bold | Multiple surfaces stepping down toward LR | Dramatic, multiple zones, complex |
| C8 | Pass-Through Window | AVAILABLE | Moderate | Opening cut into bar structure for serving/visual connection | Less counter, more connection |
| C9 | Breakfast Nook Integration | AVAILABLE | Moderate | Bar curves into built-in bench seating on one end | Dedicated dining spot, takes floor space |
| C10 | Peninsula vs Island | AVAILABLE | Bold | Freestanding island instead of attached peninsula | Requires space, different traffic flow |
